From Architecture to Cuisine

Chef Mario Pagán is a renowned culinary innovator and passionate advocate for Puerto Rican cuisine. With over 20 years of experience, he has played a pivotal role in redefining and modernizing the island's culinary traditions.

Born and raised in Puerto Rico, his early love for cooking was inspired by the women in his family. Though he initially pursued architecture in Miami, his passion led him to study at Johnson & Wales University and work under acclaimed chefs.

His architectural background heavily influences his approach to food—viewing plating and flavor composition through the lens of structure, texture, and balance. Every dish is a carefully constructed masterpiece.

EARLY CAREER

Johnson & Wales University

After studying architecture in Miami, Mario followed his true passion to culinary school, training under renowned chefs and mastering the foundations of culinary artistry.

BREAKTHROUGH

Chayote Restaurant

Purchased and transformed the iconic Chayote Restaurant, establishing his reputation as a pioneer of "Nueva Mesa Latina" - a modern interpretation of Latin cuisine.

EXPANSION

Restaurant Empire

Launched several acclaimed restaurants including MARIO PAGÁN, RAYA, LA CENTRAL in Puerto Rico, and CHAYOTE Barrio Kitchen in Florida—his first U.S.-based venture.

TODAY

Culinary Ambassador

Recognized by the James Beard Foundation, appeared on Food Network's "NEXT IRON CHEF," and continues to elevate Puerto Rico's gastronomic profile on the global stage.

Nueva Mesa Boricua

"My cooking blends traditional Puerto Rican flavors with global influences, creating the foundation of the 'Nueva Mesa Boricua' movement—where heritage meets innovation on every plate."

Chef Mario's philosophy extends beyond the kitchen. He champions sustainability, supports local agriculture, and has earned recognition from the James Beard Foundation for his commitment to elevating Puerto Rican cuisine and culture around the world.
